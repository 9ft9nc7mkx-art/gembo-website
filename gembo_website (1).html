My Story

From the mountains of Bhutan to the trading floors of Chicago — my path has been anything but conventional.

I grew up in Bhutan, a small Himalayan kingdom known more for its Gross National Happiness than its financial markets. Pursuing the International Baccalaureate at Mahindra United World College in Pune, India, I was surrounded by students from over 80 countries — an experience that shaped how I see the world and the people in it.

That global curiosity brought me to Lake Forest College in Illinois, where I found my passion at the intersection of economics, data, and finance. From reconciling books for a hospitality company in Thimphu, to modeling cross-border payment flows for Bhutan's tourism sector, to building quantitative strategies in Chicago — every role has added a new lens.

I believe the best analysts aren't just technically sharp — they understand context, culture, and the human side of capital. Three countries, three continents, one consistent thread: making sense of complexity.
